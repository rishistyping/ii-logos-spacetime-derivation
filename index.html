Why this matters

The project asks a single sharp question: can a paper-grade geometric story be separated into the part we can prove with Lean and the part we still keep as interpretation? The paper argues that time direction and expansion behavior are connected to algebraic structure from a curved spatial starting point and a signature change.

What is proved now

A Lean-proved algebraic spine gives exact matrix identities, branch signatures, and eigendirections.

What is pending

Physical readings such as horizon interpretation, redshift, and the shared-sign thesis are kept explicit as interpretation unless added as theorem packets later.

What this companion is

An editorial/interactive surface with no new proof authority: just this web page, verified artifacts, and links to repository truths.

Six-step story

1. Positive metric starts the story Space is built from a dot product where distances are sums of squares and no direction is preferred.
2. Curvature comes from translation non-commutativity The relation [P_a, P_b] = λ J_ab classifies maximally symmetric branches by the sign of λ.
3. Finiteness selects the positive branch The sphere branch is compact and finite under the natural invariant measure, giving the spatial finite starting point.
4. Wick/ signature change gives de Sitter form A fixed spatial algebra maps into a spacetime-signature algebra with the same curvature sign parameter and Λ = 3λ in four dimensions.
5. Time evolution is a 2×2 matrix On each (K_i, P_i) plane, A(λ) = [[0,-λ],[-1,0]] gives exact determinant/trace/charpoly/eigenstructure in Lean.
6. Sign language versus interpretation boundary The eigenspaces and split quadratic form are algebraically certified; light-cone, horizon, and arrow-of-time conclusions remain interpretation.
